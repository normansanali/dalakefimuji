The top 10 Winningest Franchises in NHL History

July 2024 Â· 4 minute read

When it comes to hockey, the National Hockey League (NHL) is the premier professional league in North America. Over the years, numerous franchises have left their mark on the league with their success on the ice. In this article, we will explore the top 10 winningest franchises in NHL history, highlighting their accomplishments and contributions to the sport.

1. Montreal Canadiens

The Montreal Canadiens are undoubtedly the most successful franchise in NHL history. Established in 1909, the Canadiens have won a record 24 Stanley Cup championships, with their most recent victory coming in 1993. Led by legendary players such as Maurice Richard, Jean Beliveau, and Guy Lafleur, the Canadiens have dominated the league for decades, earning them the top spot on this list.

2. Toronto Maple Leafs

The Toronto Maple Leafs, founded in 1917, have a rich history in the NHL. With 13 Stanley Cup championships to their name, the Maple Leafs stand as one of the most storied franchises in the league. Despite a lengthy championship drought since their last win in 1967, the Maple Leafs continue to be a beloved and successful team in the NHL.

3. Detroit Red Wings

The Detroit Red Wings have been a force to be reckoned with in the NHL for many years. With 11 Stanley Cup championships, the Red Wings have consistently been one of the league's top teams. Led by superstars like Gordie Howe and Steve Yzerman, the Red Wings have built a legacy of success and have a passionate fan base that supports them.

4. Boston Bruins

The Boston Bruins have a long and storied history in the NHL, with six Stanley Cup championships to their name. Known for their physical style of play, the Bruins have been a perennial contender in the league. Players like Bobby Orr, Phil Esposito, and Ray Bourque have left an indelible mark on the franchise and contributed to their success over the years.

5. Chicago Blackhawks

The Chicago Blackhawks have experienced a resurgence in recent years, but their success stretches back decades. With six Stanley Cup championships, the Blackhawks have established themselves as one of the top franchises in the league. Led by stars like Bobby Hull, Stan Mikita, and Jonathan Toews, the Blackhawks have thrilled fans with their exciting brand of hockey.

6. Edmonton Oilers

The Edmonton Oilers burst onto the scene in the 1980s with their high-powered offense led by Wayne Gretzky, Mark Messier, and Jari Kurri. With five Stanley Cup championships, the Oilers dominated the league during this era. Although they have struggled in recent years, the Oilers' past success cements their place as one of the winningest franchises in NHL history.

7. New York Islanders

The New York Islanders enjoyed a dominant run in the early 1980s, winning four consecutive Stanley Cup championships from 1980 to 1983. Led by Mike Bossy, Bryan Trottier, and Denis Potvin, the Islanders were a force to be reckoned with. While they haven't reached the same heights in recent years, their past success places them among the top winning franchises in NHL history.

8. Pittsburgh Penguins

The Pittsburgh Penguins have had their fair share of success since joining the NHL in 1967. With five Stanley Cup championships, the Penguins have been fortunate to have some of the game's greatest players, including Mario Lemieux and Sidney Crosby, don their jersey. Their consistent success has made them a respected franchise in the league.

9. New York Rangers

The New York Rangers have a loyal and passionate fan base that has supported the team through thick and thin. With four Stanley Cup championships, the Rangers have had their moments of glory, particularly in 1994 when they won their first championship in 54 years. The Rangers' history and success make them one of the top franchises in the NHL.

10. Colorado Avalanche

The Colorado Avalanche burst onto the scene in 1995 after relocating from Quebec City. Led by superstars like Joe Sakic and Peter Forsberg, the Avalanche quickly established themselves as a force in the NHL. With two Stanley Cup championships, the Avalanche have solidified their place among the top winning franchises in the league.

The NHL has seen many successful franchises throughout its history. The top 10 winningest franchises, including the Montreal Canadiens, Toronto Maple Leafs, and Detroit Red Wings, have left an indelible mark on the league with their success on the ice. These franchises have built legacies of excellence and have contributed to the growth and popularity of hockey. As the league continues to evolve, it will be exciting to see which teams will join this prestigious list in the future.
