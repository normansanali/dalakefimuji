Bill Mumy Biography

Bill Mumy was born in San Gabriel, California, to childbirth instructor Muriel Gertrude Mumy and cattle rancher Charles Wiliam Mumy. Mumy’s ethnicity is French-Canadian, despite his nationality being American.

Bill Mumy Age, Height, Weight

Bill Mumy was born on February 1, 1954, and is 69 years old as of 2023. He stands 5 feet 7 inches tall and weighs 69 kg.

Career

Mumy began his career as a six-year-old, appearing on television in family drama series and other on-screen roles. Among his many childhood appearances was his role as Willy in the 1960 episode of the NBC-TV family drama series National Velvet. In November 1961, he began three original episodes of Twilight Zone: ‘It’s a Good Life’ on CBS-TV.

He appears as a child who, using his psychic abilities, terrorizes his town. Later in the revival series, he is seen playing the same role with his daughter Liliana in the ‘It’s Still a Good Life’ episode.

Mumy was cast in Alfred Hitchcock Presents on CBS-TV in ‘The Door Without a Key,’ where he played his father in ‘It’s a Good Life,’ in 1961.

In the same year as Marta Kristen, he appeared in the episode ‘Bang! You’re Dead’ as little Jackie. In 1962, he was cast as Mark Murdock in the ‘Keep an Eye on Santa Claus’ episode of the ABC-TV drama series ‘Going My Way,’ alongside Gene Kelly.

Mumy was reportedly the first choice to play Eddie Munster in the 1964 CBS situation comedy ‘The Munsters,’ but his parents objected to the extensive makeup requirements. Butch Patrick was cast in this role instead. Mumy played Will Robinson in the ‘Lost in Space’ filming from 1965 to 1968.

Mumy has also narrated over 50 episodes of the ‘Arts and Entertainment Channel’s Biography’ series as a voice actor. His voice can also be heard on Scooby-Doo, Batman, Little Wizard Adventures, Ren and Stimpy, The Oz Kids, and Disney’s Buzz Lightyear from Star Command and Doc McStuffins.

He has provided the voiceover for national commercials for companies such as Farmers Insurance, Ford, Twix, Oscar Mayer, Mcdonald’s, and Bud Ice.

Mumy is a musician who excels at bass guitar, banjo, harmonica, keyboards, mandolin, and percussion. In music, he is credited for songs he recorded and toured with Shaun Cassidy, as well as The Be Five band he founded. Mumy has managed over 18 films, 400 television series, and numerous commercials since beginning his career.
