Who is Billy Gunn?

Billy Gunn aka Monty “Kip” Sopp is an American professional wrestler and coach. He is currently signed to All Elite Wrestling (AEW), where he is a member of The Acclaimed, along with Max Caster and Anthony Bowens, as one third of the current AEW World Trios Champions. This is his first reign as champion.

His prior roles as a trainer for NXT and a coach for WWE’s Tough Enough, as well as his stints in World Wrestling Federation/Entertainment (WWF/E) from 1993 to 2004 and 2012 to 2015, are what made him well-known.

From 2005 until 2009, he also competed for Total Nonstop Action Wrestling (TNA, currently known as Impact Wrestling).

In addition, he has won two WWF Hardcore titles and one WWF Intercontinental title, for a total of 14 WWE championships. As a member of D-Generation X, he was inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame in 2019 and also won the 1999 King of the Ring competition.

Billy Gunn Career

Billy Gunn made his WWF debut on the Raw show on May 17, 1993, teaming up with his brother Bart to defeat Glenn Ruth and Tony Vadja. Together with The Steiner Brothers, the pair who are currently known as The Smoking Gunns overcame Money Inc. and The Headshrinkers in an eight-man tag match at King of the Ring to make their pay-per-view debut.

Gunn defeated Hardcore Holly to win the Hardcore Championship. Holly defeated Gunn for the title at WrestleMania XV in a Triple Threat match that also featured Al Snow. The New Age Outlaws later got back together to triumph at Backlash over Owen Hart and Jeff Jarrett.

Following Backlash, Gunn broke away from D-Generation X and sided with Triple H and Chyna. Gunn won a match at Over the Edge over Road Dogg, his old partner. After that, Gunn defeated Kane, Ken Shamrock, and X-Pac, his old buddy, to win the King of the Ring tournament.

He joined Chuck Palumbo in a Tag Team match when he returned in 2002. It appeared that the two were really fond of one another. In addition to successfully defending the title at WrestleMania X-8, they won the Tag Team Gold twice.

In 2000, Billy Gunn starred in the sitcom “Sabrina the Teenage Witch.” He appeared in the action comedy film “The Other Guys” in 2010 but received no credit. He appeared in the movie “Double Fury” in 2012.
