His educational background

Floyd Mayweather Jr career

Floyd won the National Golden Gloves championship from 1993 to 1996 and has 84-8 records. He participated in the Atlanta Olympics where he won a bronze medal in the featherweight division in 1996.

After Floyd’s fourteenth professional fight, his father became his trainer as he was released from prison. He won his first world title the WBC Super Featherweight Championship in 1998 when he was in his second year of boxing and was also the first U.S Olympian to win a world title.

He successfully defended his title three times against Carlos Rico, Justin Juuko and Carlos Gerena.

In 2000, appointed James Prince as his manager and defended his title the fifth time. He continued his career till 2009. He is also the owner of the boxing gym called Mayweather Boxing Club.

Floyd Mayweather Jr awards

Floyd has been showered with a plethora of prestigious wards such as Michigan State and National State Golden Glove Champion Award and won three times.

He also won the bronze medal at the Atlanta Olympics Featherweight Championship in 1996. In 1998 and 2007. He won the International Boxing Fighter of the Year Award and The Ring Fighter of the Year Award.

Floyd also won the BWAA Fighter of the year award thrice and the Boxing Hall of Fame and ESPN Fighter of the Year Award and then won the Best Fighter ESPY award six times.

He was The Ring number 1 Pound for Pound from 2005 to 2008. He is the winner of Guinness World Records three times.
