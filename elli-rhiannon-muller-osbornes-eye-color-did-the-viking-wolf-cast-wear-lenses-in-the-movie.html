Viking Wolf: Is Gudbrand the Grim Based on a Real Viking?

The history of Gudbrand the Grim plays a crucial role in framing the events of Viking Wolf. The journey the monster took with the Viking leader to reach Norway is said to be the inspiration for the movie’s title. As a result, the movie practically establishes Gudbrand the Grim as a figure in Norse mythology. The figure, nevertheless, doesn’t seem to be based on a historical Viking. There is a story about a guy by the name of Gudbrand in Norse legend. The word is derived from the Old Norse word Guðbrandr. Its translation is “God’s Sword.”

Gudbrand is a farmer rather than a Viking in the Norse folktale “Gudbrand on the Hill-side.” Gudbrand and his wife are at the center of the narrative as they deal with a series of upsetting incidents on their farm. Gudbrand is seen as an impulsive person whose choices frequently get him into problems. The positivity that can be found in any circumstance is the story’s main subject. As a consequence, it is clear that the Gudbrand from the folktale has nothing in common with the character from the movie.

The legends of Haakon Sigurdsson, better known as Haakon Jarl, the king of Norway, speak of a real Viking called Gudbrand. However, as Haakon lived in the tenth century and was slain by a werewolf, it seems improbable that Gudbrand from the movie is based on this Viking.

Additionally, we were unable to uncover any information on the 11th-century activities of a Viking general by the name of Gudbrand. Therefore, it is safe to assume that the producers invented Gudbrand the Grim as a mythical Viking. Gudbrand’s tale, however, adds an intriguing new dimension to werewolf mythology.
