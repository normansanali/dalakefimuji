Understanding Southpaw Boxing

Before delving into whether Otto Wallin is a southpaw, it is important to understand what being a southpaw means in the context of boxing. In boxing, a southpaw is a boxer who fights in an orthodox stance, meaning their right hand and right foot are forward. This is opposite to the conventional stance, where the left hand and left foot are forward.

This is consistent with the orthodox style, where the left hand is used for jabs and setting up combinations.

Advantages of Fighting Southpaw

Switching to a southpaw stance can provide several advantages for a boxer. Firstly, it can confuse the opponent, as they are accustomed to fighting against orthodox boxers. The change in stance can disrupt their rhythm and make it difficult for them to anticipate the boxer's movements and punches. Secondly, fighting in a southpaw stance can create different angles of attack, allowing for unique combinations and openings to exploit.
