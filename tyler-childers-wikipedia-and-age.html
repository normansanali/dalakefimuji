Tyler Childers Wikipedia and age

August 2024 · 3 minute read

Following Tyler Childers’s announcement of his new album, Rustin’ in the Rain scheduled for release on September 8th, we are going to focus on his Wikipedia and age.

Before delving more into his family, let us see who Tyler is. Tyler Childers is an American singer and songwriter. His music is a mix of neotraditional country, bluegrass, and folk.

He released his breakthrough album Purgatory in August 2017. Childers has released five studio albums and a number of EPs and singles.

Tyler Childers was raised in Lawrence County, Kentucky, where he was born. His mother is a nurse, while his father worked in the coal business.

He gained singing experience in the church choir, where he sang. When he was about 13 years old, he began to play the guitar and compose songs.

He graduated in 2009 from Paintsville High School in the neighbourhood of Paintsville in Johnson County, Kentucky. From Johnson County, too, are fellow country performers Chris Stapleton, Loretta Lynn (Butcher Hollow), and Crystal Gayle.

Childers attended Bluegrass Community and Technical College for several semesters while also taking classes at Western Kentucky University for a semester. He left college and worked odd jobs for a spell while seeking a career in music.

In Huntington, West Virginia, and Lexington, Kentucky, Childers debuted.[8] Childers’ debut album, Bottles And Bibles, was published in 2011 when he was 19 years old. Additionally, he has published two EPs that were made in 2013 and played on Lexington’s Red Barn Radio.

Following the success of his album Purgatory, the two EPs were subsequently combined and published as Live on Red Barn Radio I & II, which peaked at No. 5 on the Heatseekers Albums.[9] He performed with The Food Stamps as his backing band.

His breakthrough album Purgatory, which was published on August 4, 2017, brought him his first success. Sturgill Simpson and David Ferguson produced the album, which was recorded in Nashville’s The Butcher Shoppe.

Along with Miles Miller on drums, Stuart Duncan on fiddle, and Russ Paul on other instruments, Simpson performed backing vocals and played guitar on the record. It made its debut at No. 1 on the Heatseekers Albums chart by Billboard, No. 17 on the Country Albums chart, and No. 4 on the Americana/Folk Albums chart.

Childers wed Senora May, a Kentucky native and fellow musician, in 2015. They shared the news that they were expecting their first child in April 2022.
